Trade[XYZ] lists perpetual futures on major global equity index benchmarks. Rather than tracking a single stock, these contracts let you gain leveraged exposure to broad market movements — US tech, the S&P 500, Japanese equities, Korean equities, Chinese equities, and implied volatility — without holding the underlying basket or managing futures rolls yourself.

## Oracle design

Equity index oracles use a **two-source model**: spot index value plus discounted futures price.

During regular hours, the cash index is published directly and serves as the primary oracle input. During extended sessions — when the underlying cash index is not published — Trade[XYZ] infers the spot price from active futures contracts using the cost-of-carry model:

```
S = F × e^(-(r-q)T)
```

Where `S` is the inferred spot price, `F` is the current futures price, `r` is the risk-free rate, `q` is the dividend yield, and `T` is time to expiration. This allows the oracle to remain active and well-anchored during non-cash hours without introducing a pure futures basis into the reference price.

<Note>
VIX is an exception to the two-source model. The VIX oracle uses the cash index directly — no cost-of-carry conversion is needed. See the [VIX perpetual page](/asset-directory/volatility) for details.
</Note>

## Roll schedule

All equity index contracts are quarterly, expiring in March (H), June (M), September (U), and December (Z).

**Roll rule**: Trade[XYZ] rolls to the next contract at **10:00 ET on the Monday before expiration week**. The roll happens during the live cash session to minimize slippage and ensure orderly transition.

<Tip>
Check the consolidated roll calendar for exact roll timestamps for each upcoming contract month. CME equity index futures settle on the third Friday of the contract month.
</Tip>

### XYZ100 — US Large-Cap Tech & Growth Index

**Coin**: `xyz:XYZ100` | **Category**: indices | **Keywords**: nasdaq, qqq

XYZ100 tracks a modified market-capitalization-weighted index of 100 of the largest, most actively-traded, non-financial companies listed on a US stock exchange. It serves as a benchmark for large-cap US technology and growth equities — the same index tracked by the popular QQQ ETF.

**Underlying contract**: CME E-mini Nasdaq-100 (NQ)

| Specification | Detail |
|--------------|--------|
| Contract code | NQ |
| Exchange | CME Globex |
| Contract unit | $20 × Nasdaq-100 Index |
| Price quotation | Index points |
| Minimum tick | 0.25 points = $5.00/contract |
| Contract months | Quarterly (Mar, Jun, Sep, Dec) |
| Settlement | Cash, against SOQ |
| Oracle coverage | ~23h (CME Globex hours) |

XYZ100 tracks the Nasdaq-100, so technology, consumer discretionary, and communication services companies dominate the index weight. Healthcare and industrials have smaller representation. Financial companies are excluded by construction.

### SP500 — S&P 500 Index

**Coin**: `xyz:SP500` | **Category**: indices | **Keywords**: es, spx

SP500 tracks the S&P 500 Index, a market-capitalization-weighted index of 500 leading publicly traded companies in the US. It is widely regarded as the best single gauge of large-cap US equities and serves as the benchmark for most institutional equity portfolios globally.

**Underlying contract**: CME E-mini S&P 500 (ES)

| Specification | Detail |
|--------------|--------|
| Contract code | ES |
| Exchange | CME Globex |
| Contract months | Quarterly (Mar, Jun, Sep, Dec) |
| Settlement | Cash |
| Oracle coverage | ~23h (CME Globex hours) |

The S&P 500 is broader than the Nasdaq-100, covering all 11 GICS sectors. Technology is the largest single sector by weight, but financials, healthcare, consumer staples, and industrials all have meaningful representation.

### JP225 — Japan 225 (Nikkei 225)

**Coin**: `xyz:JP225` | **Category**: indices | **Keywords**: japan, nikkei

JP225 tracks a JPY-based, price-weighted index of 225 leading Japanese companies, serving as the primary benchmark for the Japanese equity market. Unlike most modern indices, the Nikkei 225 is price-weighted rather than market-cap-weighted — similar in construction to the Dow Jones Industrial Average.

**Underlying contracts**: Nikkei 225 futures on OSE (JNK) and CME (NIY)

| Specification | Detail |
|--------------|--------|
| Primary exchange | OSE (Osaka Exchange) |
| CME listing | NIY (USD-denominated mini) |
| Index currency | JPY |
| Oracle coverage | ~21h (OSE) + ~23h (CME) |

**Important**: The oracle follows the index's native JPY level without FX conversion. JP225 is quoted in index points, not USD. If the Nikkei falls from 38,000 to 37,000, your P&L reflects that 1,000-point move regardless of USD/JPY movements.

### KR200 — Korea 200 (KOSPI 200)

**Coin**: `xyz:KR200` | **Category**: indices | **Keywords**: korea, kospi

KR200 tracks a KRW-based, capitalization-weighted index of 200 large-cap South Korean companies, serving as the primary benchmark for the South Korean equity market. The KOSPI 200 covers approximately 93% of the total market capitalization of the Korea Exchange.

| Specification | Detail |
|--------------|--------|
| Exchange | KRX (Korea Exchange) |
| Index currency | KRW |
| Oracle coverage | ~19h (KRX day + night sessions) |

**Important**: The oracle follows the index's native KRW level without FX conversion. KR200 is quoted in index points. P&L is settled in USD based on the contract's USD denomination, but the underlying index level is tracked in KRW without a continuous FX adjustment.

### CSI300 — China 300 (CSI 300)

**Coin**: `xyz:CSI300` | **Category**: indices

CSI300 tracks the CSI 300 Index, a capitalization-weighted index of 300 large-cap companies listed on the Shanghai Stock Exchange (SSE) and Shenzhen Stock Exchange (SZSE). It is the primary benchmark for Chinese mainland equities (A-shares).

| Specification | Detail |
|--------------|--------|
| Primary exchange | CFFEX (China Financial Futures Exchange) |
| Extended hours proxy | SGX A50 futures |
| Oracle coverage | ~20h (SGX A50 proxy for extended hours) |

During extended hours when CFFEX is closed, the oracle uses SGX-listed FTSE China A50 futures as a proxy to maintain pricing continuity.

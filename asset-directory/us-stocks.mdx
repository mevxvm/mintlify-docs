Trade[XYZ] offers perpetual futures on individual US equities listed on NYSE and NASDAQ. Each contract references a real-time spot price — there are no roll mechanics, expiration dates, or basis adjustments to manage. You simply go long or short, and the oracle tracks the live stock price continuously throughout covered trading hours.

## Exchange coverage

US equity perpetuals cover the full regular trading session (RTH) plus extended hours (pre-market and after-hours) on both NYSE and NASDAQ.

| Session | Hours (ET) | Notes |
|---------|-----------|-------|
| Pre-market | 4:00 AM – 9:30 AM | Extended hours; liquidity varies |
| Regular (RTH) | 9:30 AM – 4:00 PM | Full coverage, tightest spreads |
| After-hours | 4:00 PM – 8:00 PM | Extended hours; liquidity varies |

<Note>
Extended hours oracle quality depends on the data providers active for each symbol. During RTH, multiple providers contribute high-quality feeds. During pre-market and after-hours, fewer market makers are active and bid-ask spreads are wider.
</Note>

## Extended-hours oracle coverage

Trade[XYZ] aggregates price data from multiple independent providers to maintain oracle quality across all sessions. During regular trading hours (RTH), all providers contribute high-quality, tightly arbitraged feeds. During pre-market and after-hours, fewer market makers are active, which can widen effective spreads and reduce oracle precision.

<Note>
Extended-hours oracle quality varies by symbol. High-volume stocks like TSLA, NVDA, and AAPL maintain better extended-hours pricing than lower-volume names.
</Note>

## Foreign shares listed in the US

Some assets in this list — BABA (Alibaba) and TSM (TSMC) — are American Depositary Shares (ADS) of foreign companies trading on US exchanges. Because they are US-listed and priced in USD, no FX conversion is applied. You trade them exactly like any other US equity perpetual.

<Tip>
ETFs like EWJ and EWY are US-listed funds that provide diversified exposure to Japanese and South Korean equities respectively. They trade on US exchanges in USD and require no FX conversion.
</Tip>

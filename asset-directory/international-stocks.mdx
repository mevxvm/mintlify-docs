Trade[XYZ] lists perpetual futures on select equities from Korea and Japan. Unlike US-listed stocks, these are **composite** contracts: the oracle converts the underlying stock's local-currency price to USD at the prevailing spot FX rate. You trade in USD with no need to hold or manage foreign currency directly.

<Note>
Chinese stocks listed on SSE, SZSE, and HKEX have limited oracle coverage of approximately 4 hours during daytime hours only. Check the current market status page for live availability before trading Chinese equities.
</Note>

## Korean equity perpetuals

All Korean equity perpetuals are KRW → USD composite listings. The oracle converts the underlying stock's Korean won price to US dollars at the prevailing USD/KRW spot rate in real time.

### Trading venues

| Venue | Type | Session (KST) |
|-------|------|--------------|
| KRX / KOSPI | Primary exchange | 09:00 – 15:30 |
| Nextrade (NXT) | ATS (alternative trading system) | 08:00 – 20:00 |

Nextrade extends coverage significantly beyond the primary KRX session — from 08:00 to 20:00 KST — giving you roughly 12 hours of liquidity per weekday.

## Japanese equity perpetuals

All Japanese equity perpetuals are JPY → USD composite listings. The oracle converts the underlying stock's yen price to US dollars at the prevailing USD/JPY spot rate in real time.

### Trading venues

| Venue | Type | Session (JST) |
|-------|------|--------------|
| TSE (Tokyo Stock Exchange) | Primary exchange | 09:00 – 15:30 |
| Japanext PTS | Night-session ATS | 16:30 – 23:59 |

Japanext's night session runs from 16:30 to 23:59 JST, bridging into early US trading hours and substantially extending your access to Japanese equity pricing.

## Oracle and FX conversion

For all international equity perpetuals, the oracle price you see in Trade[XYZ] is always denominated in USD. The conversion uses the prevailing spot USD/KRW or USD/JPY rate at the time of each oracle update. You are implicitly exposed to FX movements in addition to the underlying equity price — a falling KRW or JPY will reduce the USD value of Korean or Japanese positions even if the local share price is unchanged.

<Tip>
If you want pure Korean or Japanese equity exposure without FX risk, consider pairing an international equity perpetual with a position in the corresponding FX perpetual (e.g., `xyz:JPY` for yen exposure).
</Tip>

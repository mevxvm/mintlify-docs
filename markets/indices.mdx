Index perps use a two-source oracle — combining the live spot index value with discounted futures prices — so pricing stays accurate even during extended sessions when the underlying exchange is closed.

## Roll mechanics

All equity index contracts are **quarterly**, expiring in March, June, September, and December. Rolls happen instantaneously at **10:00 ET on the Monday before expiration week**.

Because the oracle converts futures prices to spot via the cost-of-carry model before and after the roll, the spread between the outgoing and incoming contract is negligible. You experience no price gap at the roll.

<Note>
  You do not need to take any action at roll time. Trade[XYZ] handles the transition automatically, and your open position continues without interruption.
</Note>

## Oracle methodology: two-source design

Equity index perps use a two-source oracle:

1. **Spot index** — the live published level of the underlying index during exchange hours.
2. **Discounted futures** — when the spot index is unavailable (outside exchange hours), the oracle infers the spot price from the current futures price using the cost-of-carry model:

$$S = F \cdot e^{-(r-q)T}$$

Where:
- `S` is the implied spot index level
- `F` is the current futures price
- `r` is the risk-free rate
- `q` is the dividend yield
- `T` is time to futures expiration (in years)

This design ensures continuous, economically grounded pricing during extended sessions — even when the underlying exchange is closed.

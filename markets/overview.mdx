Every market references real-time prices through a transparent, multi-source oracle, so you always know what drives the price you're trading. There are no expiries to manage — positions stay open until you close them or funding adjusts your cost of carry.

## Roll calendar

Assets that reference traditional futures contracts must be rolled before expiration. Two roll mechanisms are in use across the platform:

**Equity index roll** — Contracts roll instantaneously at 10:00 ET on the Monday before expiration week. Because prices are converted to spot via the cost-of-carry model before and after the roll, the spread between the outgoing and incoming contracts is negligible.

**Commodity roll** — Contracts transition over business days 5–9 of the roll month at 17:30 ET, using a 5-step linear weighting. This graduated approach minimises price deviation between the outgoing and incoming contracts, consistent with S&P GSCI and Bloomberg Commodity Index methodology.

<Note>
  Stock and FX perpetuals have no roll mechanics — they reference real-time spot prices directly, so there is no contract expiry to manage.
</Note>

## International equities

Foreign stocks listed on Trade[XYZ] are **composite** instruments. The oracle prices each foreign share in its native currency, then converts to USD at the prevailing FX rate:

- Korean stocks (KRX/Nextrade ATS): KRW → USD
- Japanese stocks (TSE/Japanext PTS): JPY → USD

This means your PnL on Samsung (SMSN) or SoftBank (SOFTBANK) reflects both the share price move and any USD/KRW or USD/JPY move over the same period.

Trade[XYZ] lists perpetual futures on commodity assets across three sectors: energy, precious metals, and base metals. Each commodity perp references an underlying exchange-traded futures contract. Because futures contracts expire, Trade[XYZ] uses a 5-step linear roll transition to move from the outgoing contract to the incoming one smoothly — minimising price impact and aligning with established commodity index methodology.

## Roll mechanism

Commodity perps must be rolled as the underlying futures contract approaches expiration. Trade[XYZ] uses a **5-step linear transition** over business days 5–9 of the roll month, executed at **17:30 ET** each day.

| Business day | Outgoing contract weight | Incoming contract weight |
|---|---|---|
| Day 5 | 80% | 20% |
| Day 6 | 60% | 40% |
| Day 7 | 40% | 60% |
| Day 8 | 20% | 80% |
| Day 9 | 0% | 100% |

The new contract weight distribution takes effect at the open of the next trading session after each daily step. This graduated approach minimises the price deviation between contracts and aligns with the methodology used by the S&P GSCI and Bloomberg Commodity Index (BCOM).

<Note>
  You do not need to manually roll your position. Trade[XYZ] handles the roll automatically. The linear transition reduces the basis impact you would otherwise experience from a single instantaneous roll.
</Note>

## Price sources

Precious metals (Gold, Silver, Platinum, Palladium) use a spot-based oracle — prices are derived from real-time spot metal prices rather than the front-month futures contract alone. Energy and base metal perps use the underlying futures contract price, transitioned smoothly across roll periods.

<Info>
  Commodity perp prices are denominated in USD. WTI and Brent are priced per barrel; Henry Hub and TTF per MMBtu; Gold, Silver, Platinum, and Palladium per troy ounce; Copper per pound; and Aluminium per metric ton.
</Info>

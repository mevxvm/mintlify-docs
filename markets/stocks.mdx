Trade[XYZ] lists perpetual futures on individual equities from US and international exchanges. Unlike traditional stock futures, stock perps reference real-time spot prices directly — there are no roll mechanics, no expiry dates, and no basis risk from futures rollover. You hold a position that tracks the underlying share price continuously.

## US equities

## International equities

Foreign equity perps are **composite** instruments. The oracle prices each share in its native currency and converts to USD at the prevailing spot FX rate. Your PnL reflects both the equity price move and the USD FX move over the same period.

<Warning>
  Because foreign equity perps convert native currency prices to USD in real time, your exposure includes both the equity and the FX rate. A move in USD/KRW or USD/JPY will affect your position's USD value even if the underlying share price is unchanged.
</Warning>

<Note>
  Stock perps have no roll mechanics. There is no futures contract underneath — the oracle references the real-time spot price of the underlying equity, so you never need to manage a roll or track a basis.
</Note>

## How stock perp prices are determined

Each stock perp tracks the real-time spot price of the underlying share. During US regular trading hours, the oracle sources prices directly from NYSE and NASDAQ feeds. During extended hours and overnight, the oracle continues to reference available price feeds, including ATS and PTS sessions where applicable.

<Tip>
  Use EWY (iShares MSCI South Korea ETF) or EWJ (iShares MSCI Japan ETF) for broad regional exposure without the individual-stock FX composite mechanics.
</Tip>
